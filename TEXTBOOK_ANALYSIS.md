## WEIRD EDGE PROBLEMS IDENTIFIED

These are non-standard problem types that students often struggle with:

### 1. Absolute Value = Negative Number
**Example:** |2x-5| = -3
**Edge:** Absolute value always ≥ 0, so no solution (∅)
**Frequency:** Appears in 1.6 practice, problems like #12

### 2. Two Absolute Values Equal
**Example:** |2x-7| = |4x+6|
**Edge:** Two cases: 2x-7 = 4x+6 OR 2x-7 = -(4x+6)
**Frequency:** 1.6 practice #31-36

### 3. Age Problem - Sum Given Now
**Example:** "The sum of Nicole and Kristin's age is 32. In two years Nicole will be three times as old as Kristin."
**Edge:** Total goes above "Now" column, second person = Total - x
**Frequency:** Age problems #9-12

### 4. Age Problem - "How Many Years Until"
**Example:** "Louis is 26, her daughter is 4. In how many years will Louis be double her daughter's age?"
**Edge:** Unknown time t, add t to both ages
**Frequency:** Age problems #13-16

### 5. Systems - Same Line (Infinite Solutions)
**Example:** 2x-6y=12 and 3x-9y=18
**Edge:** After solving for y, both equations identical
**Frequency:** 4.1 Example 169

### 6. Systems - Parallel (No Solution)
**Example:** y=(3/2)x-4 and y=(3/2)x+1
**Edge:** Same slope, different intercepts
**Frequency:** 4.1 Example 168

### 7. Long Division - Missing Terms
**Example:** (2x³+42-4x) ÷ (x+3)
**Edge:** Must add 0x² term before dividing
**Frequency:** 5.7 Example 261

### 8. Variation with Squared Variables
**Example:** "Area varies directly as square of radius"
**Edge:** A/r² = k, not A/r = k
**Frequency:** 1.7 Example 101

### 9. Compound Inequalities - No Overlap
**Example:** x > 5 AND x < 2
**Edge:** No solution (∅)
**Frequency:** 3.2 discussion

### 10. Compound Inequalities - Full Overlap
**Example:** x < 3 OR x > 1
**Edge:** All real numbers (R)
**Frequency:** 3.2 discussion

### 11. Rational Equations - Extraneous Solutions
**Example:** Solve rational equation, solution makes denominator zero
**Edge:** Must check all solutions in original equation
**Frequency:** 7.7

### 12. Complex Fractions with Multiple Operations
**Example:** [(x²-1)/(2x-4)] · [(x²-4)/(x²-x-2)] ÷ [(x²+x-2)/(3x-6)]
**Edge:** Factor everything first, then multiply by reciprocal, cancel
**Frequency:** 7.2 #41-44

### 13. LCD with Repeated Factors
**Example:** Find LCD of x²-10x+25 and x²-14x+45
**Edge:** (x-5)² vs (x-5)(x-9) → LCD = (x-5)²(x-9)
**Frequency:** 7.3 Example 341

### 14. Radicals - Mixed Index
**Example:** Simplify √[3]{√x}
**Edge:** Convert to rational exponents: x^(1/6)
**Frequency:** 8.7

### 15. Complex Numbers - Division
**Example:** (3+2i)/(1-4i)
**Edge:** Multiply numerator and denominator by conjugate (1+4i)
**Frequency:** 8.8

### 16. Quadratic in Form
**Example:** x⁴-5x²+4=0
**Edge:** Substitute u=x², solve u²-5u+4=0, back-substitute
**Frequency:** 9.6

### 17. Compound Interest - Solve for Time
**Example:** A=P(1+r)^t, solve for t
**Edge:** Requires logarithms: t = ln(A/P)/ln(1+r)
**Frequency:** 10.6
